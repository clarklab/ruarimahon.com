Ruari Mahon

Ruari Mahon is a New York-based consultant for style and design interests, with 15 years of industry experience. His services address identity and value proposition, market positioning and penetration, and communications. His creative work begins at the center of a brand and builds outwards, fostering collaboration in an ongoing discussion between commerce and branding.

In 2020 he founded Loughlin Joseph, a management bureau for editors and writers with roots in print and digital publishing.

From 2018–2020 he served as adjunct professor at Parsons School of Design. He has also worked with Fashion Institute of Technology, USA; Fashion Institute of Design & Merchandising, USA; Manchester Metropolitan University, UK; Aalto University, School of Arts, Design and Architecture, Finland; Lincoln University, New Zealand; Innovation Forum, UK; and Xiamen International Fashion Week, China.

Clients include CDLP, Community Clothing, FEIT, Lauren Manoogian, and Nudie Jeans. Client collaborations include BDDW, Cuixmala, Erin Considine, Grand Hotel Tremezzo, Lætitia Jacquetton, Tom of Finland Foundation, and Toogood.
